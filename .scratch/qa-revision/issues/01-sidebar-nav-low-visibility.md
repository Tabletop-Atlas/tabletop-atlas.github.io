# 01 — Main navigation sidebar is too low-contrast ("invisible")

Status: ready-for-human
Category: enhancement

## What happened

The main navigation (the persistent left sidebar with Browse / Recommend / Archive / Dashboard / Tier list / Log / Settings) blends into the surrounding dark surfaces. It reads as "a bit invisible" — hard to notice as a distinct panel and hard to scan.

## What I expected

The command-deck sidebar should stand out as a clearly delineated navigation area, with coloring that separates it from the main pane and makes the nav items easy to spot.

## Steps to reproduce

1. Open the app on any tab.
2. Look at the left sidebar: its background barely differs from the main pane, and inactive nav items have low contrast against it.

## Additional context

- The reporter wants to **try a few visual prototypes** before committing to a treatment — run this through `/prototype` (2–3 coloring/contrast variants of the sidebar) and settle the design before implementing.
- The app already has an experimental URL-param-gated theming round (theme A/B/C variant switchers, marked throwaway); prototypes could build on that mechanism or replace it. All colors flow through `--deck-*` design tokens, so candidate treatments are token-level changes.
