# 02 — Expansion cleanup: only "Feather & Flame" should exist (fold "Promo" into it), and verify spirits showing wrong expansions

Status: done
Category: bug

## What happened

Two related problems with expansions:

1. The app treats **Promo** and **Feather & Flame** as two separate expansions (both appear in the settings collection checklist, browser filters, and dashboard). Historically the promo-pack spirits were later collected into the Feather & Flame boxed expansion, so having both is a duplication.
2. Some spirits appear under the wrong expansion in the UI. Reported example: **Ember-Eyed Behemoth** appeared associated with Feather & Flame, but it belongs to Nature Incarnate.

## What I expected

- A single canonical **Feather & Flame** expansion; no separate "Promo" category anywhere (checklist, filters, dashboard, card groupings). Owner decision: Feather & Flame is the surviving name.
- Every spirit listed under its correct (wiki-verifiable) expansion.

## Steps to reproduce

1. Open Settings → the expansion collection checklist shows both "Promo" and "Feather & Flame".
2. Browse spirits filtered by expansion and compare against the official Spirit Island wiki — note any spirit whose expansion is wrong (e.g. check where Ember-Eyed Behemoth appears).

## Additional context

- **This repo's known failure mode is fabricated data — the fix must be research-backed.** Verify every reassigned spirit/card against the Spirit Island wiki (primary source), and do not guess: currently 3 spirits are tagged `Promo` and 7 tagged `Feather & Flame`; each of those 10 assignments should be individually verified during the merge. (Canonically, Feather & Flame contains 4 spirits: Heart of the Wildfire, Serpent Snapping the World's Tail, Finder of Paths Unseen, Downpour Drenches the World — a count of 7 is suspicious and needs sourcing.)
- The spirit **data** already records Ember-Eyed Behemoth as `Nature Incarnate`, so the reported sighting under Feather & Flame may be a display/filtering bug rather than a data error — the fix should reproduce what the reporter saw before touching data.
- The canonical expansion list is a frozen constant with tripwire tests (`expansionCanon.test.ts`, `dataIntegrity.test.ts`, `aspectCanon.test.ts`, alias normalizer with documented mappings). Removing `Promo` means updating the canonical list, the alias table (bare `Promo` currently stays its own entry while `Promo2`/`Promo Pack 2` already map to Feather & Flame), and re-pinning the tripwires — plus the stored user collection default.
